Phishing

Category: Human Risk Difficulty: Beginner Related: Social Engineering, MFA

Definition

Phishing is a cyberattack that uses fraudulent emails, messages, or websites to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information.

Why it matters: Phishing is one of the most common attack vectors leading to breaches.

Types

  • Spear phishing: Targeted at specific individuals.
  • Whaling: Targeting executives or high-value victims.
  • Clone phishing: Replicating legitimate messages.

Best practices

  • Verify sender addresses.
  • Hover over links before clicking.
  • Use email filtering and MFA.
