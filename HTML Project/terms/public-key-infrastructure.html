Public Key Infrastructure (PKI)

Category: Cryptography Difficulty: Advanced Related: TLS/SSL, Encryption

Definition

PKI is a framework of policies, procedures, and technologies that manage digital certificates and public‑private key pairs to establish trust online.

Why it matters: PKI enables secure communication, authentication, and digital signatures across the internet.

Components

  • Certificate Authority (CA): Issues and validates certificates.
  • Registration Authority (RA): Verifies identity before certificate issuance.
  • Digital Certificates: Bind public keys to identities.

Best practices

  • Use trusted CAs.
  • Regularly renew and revoke certificates.
  • Secure private keys with hardware modules.
