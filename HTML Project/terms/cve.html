CVE (Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures)

Category: Standards Difficulty: Beginner Related: Vulnerability, Penetration Testing

Definition

CVE is a public catalog of known cybersecurity vulnerabilities, each assigned a unique identifier for tracking and remediation.

Why it matters: CVEs provide a standardized way to share and address vulnerabilities across the industry.

Examples

  • CVE-2017-0144 (EternalBlue) exploited by WannaCry.
  • CVE-2021-44228 (Log4Shell) in Apache Log4j.
  • CVE-2019-0708 (BlueKeep) in RDP services.

Best practices

  • Monitor CVE databases regularly.
  • Prioritize patching based on CVSS scores.
  • Integrate CVE feeds into vulnerability management tools.
