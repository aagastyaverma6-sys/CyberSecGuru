TLS/SSL

Category: Transport Security Difficulty: Intermediate Related: PKI, Encryption

Definition

TLS (Transport Layer Security) and its predecessor SSL (Secure Sockets Layer) are cryptographic protocols that secure communications over networks, most notably HTTPS.

Why it matters: TLS ensures confidentiality, integrity, and authenticity of web traffic.

Key features

  • Handshake process to establish secure sessions.
  • Encryption of data in transit.
  • Certificate validation using PKI.

Best practices

  • Always use TLS 1.2 or higher.
  • Disable weak ciphers and protocols.
  • Regularly update certificates.
