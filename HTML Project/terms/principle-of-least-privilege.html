Principle of Least Privilege

Category: Access Control Difficulty: Beginner Related: Authorization, Zero Trust

Definition

The principle of least privilege ensures that users and systems are granted only the minimum access necessary to perform their tasks.

Why it matters: Reduces the attack surface and limits damage if accounts are compromised.

Examples

  • Restricting admin rights to IT staff only.
  • Limiting database queries to read-only for analysts.
  • Segmenting cloud roles by function.

Best practices

  • Review permissions regularly.
  • Use role-based access control.
  • Automate privilege revocation when roles change.
