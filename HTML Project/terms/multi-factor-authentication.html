Multi‑Factor Authentication (MFA)

Category: Access Control Difficulty: Beginner Related: Authentication, Zero Trust

Definition

MFA requires users to present two or more independent credentials to verify identity, such as something they know, have, or are.

Why it matters: MFA significantly reduces the risk of account compromise.

Factors

  • Knowledge: Passwords or PINs.
  • Possession: Tokens, smartphones.
  • Inherence: Biometrics.

Best practices

  • Use at least two strong factors.
  • Favor app-based or hardware tokens over SMS.
  • Educate users on phishing-resistant MFA.
