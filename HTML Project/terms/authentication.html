Authentication

Category: Access Control Difficulty: Beginner Related: Authorization, MFA

Definition

Authentication is the process of verifying that a user or system is who they claim to be, typically through credentials such as passwords, biometrics, or tokens.

Why it matters: Without authentication, systems cannot ensure that access requests come from legitimate sources.

Common methods

  • Password-based: Traditional but vulnerable to attacks.
  • Biometric: Fingerprints, facial recognition.
  • Token-based: Smart cards, OTPs.

Best practices

  • Use strong, unique passwords.
  • Enable MFA wherever possible.
  • Regularly review authentication logs.
