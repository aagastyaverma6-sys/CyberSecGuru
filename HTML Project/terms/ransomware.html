Ransomware

Category: Malware Difficulty: Intermediate Related: Phishing, Vulnerability

Definition

Ransomware is malicious software that encrypts a victim’s files and demands payment for decryption.

Why it matters: Ransomware can cripple organizations by locking critical data and systems.

Examples

  • WannaCry (2017) exploited SMB vulnerabilities.
  • Ryuk targeted enterprises with tailored attacks.
  • LockBit used double extortion tactics.

Best practices

  • Maintain offline backups.
  • Patch systems promptly.
  • Train users to avoid suspicious attachments.
